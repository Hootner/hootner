# NDA Usage Guide
**Quick Reference for HOOTNER NDA Template**

---

## 📋 When to Use This NDA

Use the HOOTNER NDA template when:

✅ **Partnerships & Integrations**
- Discussing API integrations with third parties
- Sharing platform architecture with potential partners
- Evaluating technology partnerships

✅ **Contractors & Consultants**
- Hiring developers with access to source code
- Engaging marketing consultants with access to user data
- Working with designers who see unreleased features

✅ **Investors & Advisors**
- Sharing financial information with potential investors
- Discussing business strategy with advisors
- Presenting product roadmaps to venture capitalists

✅ **Vendors & Service Providers**
- Cloud hosting providers with infrastructure access
- Payment processors handling financial data
- Analytics services processing user data

✅ **Job Candidates**
- Senior technical interviews requiring code review
- Executive candidates reviewing business plans
- Any candidate tour of confidential systems

## ⚡ Quick Start: 3 Steps

### Step 1: Customize the Template
1. Open `NDA_TEMPLATE.md`
2. Fill in the blanks:
   - Effective Date
   - Other Party's name and contact info
   - Select term duration (2, 3, 5 years, or indefinite)
   - Choose governing law jurisdiction
   - Specify dispute resolution location

### Step 2: Review & Adjust
Customize sections based on your needs:
- **Section 1.1**: Add/remove specific types of confidential info
- **Section 6.1**: Choose appropriate term length
- **Section 10**: HOOTNER-specific provisions (usually keep as-is)
- **Appendices**: Complete disclosure logs as needed

### Step 3: Execute
1. Send to other party for review
2. Allow 3-5 business days for review
3. Sign (electronic signatures valid)
4. Store executed copy in secure location
5. Log in internal tracking system

## 🎯 Key Sections Explained

### Section 1: Confidential Information
**What it covers**: Defines what information is protected
**Critical for**: Ensuring all proprietary info is covered
**Tip**: Be specific but broad enough to cover edge cases

### Section 2: Obligations
**What it covers**: What the receiving party must/must not do
**Critical for**: Enforceable protection requirements
**Tip**: Security measures in 2.2 are especially important

### Section 5: Return of Materials
**What it covers**: What happens when relationship ends
**Critical for**: Ensuring confidential info is properly destroyed
**Tip**: Always get written certification of destruction

### Section 8: Remedies
**What it covers**: What happens if NDA is breached
**Critical for**: Your ability to stop breaches quickly
**Tip**: Equitable relief (injunctions) is key for tech companies

### Section 10: HOOTNER-Specific
**What it covers**: Platform-specific protections
**Critical for**: AI/ML models, user privacy, content protection
**Tip**: Keep these provisions in most agreements

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Using One-Way NDA for Mutual Discussions
**Problem**: If both parties share info, you need mutual protection
**Solution**: This template is mutual by default - perfect for most cases

### ❌ Mistake 2: Forgetting to Define the Purpose
**Problem**: Vague purpose = hard to enforce
**Solution**: Be specific in the "Purpose" section (line 3 of agreement)

### ❌ Mistake 3: Term Too Short
**Problem**: 1-year terms often expire before project completes
**Solution**: Use 3 years minimum, 5 years for serious partnerships

### ❌ Mistake 4: Not Tracking Disclosures
**Problem**: Can't prove what was shared and when
**Solution**: Maintain Appendix B disclosure log religiously

### ❌ Mistake 5: Ignoring State Law Differences
**Problem**: Some states have specific NDA requirements
**Solution**: Consult local attorney for jurisdiction-specific issues

### ❌ Mistake 6: Verbal Confidentiality Agreements
**Problem**: Verbal NDAs are hard/impossible to enforce
**Solution**: ALWAYS use written agreements, even for quick discussions

### ❌ Mistake 7: Waiting Too Long to Execute
**Problem**: Sharing info before NDA is signed = no protection
**Solution**: Execute NDA BEFORE any confidential discussions

## 📂 Document Management

### Storage
- **Original Executed Copy**: Legal team secure drive
- **Working Copies**: Shared drive with restricted access
- **Backups**: Cloud backup with encryption

### Tracking
Maintain spreadsheet with:
- Party name
- Execution date
- Expiration date
- Confidential info shared
- Status (active/expired/breached)

### Renewal Process
- Set calendar reminders 60 days before expiration
- Reach out to other party 45 days before expiration
- Execute renewal or extension 30 days before expiration
- Or allow to expire and return materials per Section 5

## 🚨 Breach Response Procedure

If you discover an NDA breach:

### Step 1: Document (Immediately)
- Take screenshots
- Save evidence
- Note date/time/circumstances
- Identify what was disclosed and to whom

### Step 2: Notify (Within 24 hours)
- Send breach notice to other party via email AND certified mail
- Reference specific NDA sections violated
- Demand immediate cessation of unauthorized disclosure
- Request remediation plan

### Step 3: Legal Assessment (Within 48 hours)
- Consult with legal counsel
- Assess damages
- Evaluate need for injunction
- Consider settlement vs. litigation

### Step 4: Remediation (Within 1 week)
- Work with breaching party on remediation
- Document all remediation efforts
- Update security measures
- Notify affected parties if required by law

## 📊 NDA Checklist

Before executing an NDA, verify:

- [ ] Other party's legal name is correct
- [ ] Contact information is current
- [ ] Purpose is clearly defined
- [ ] Term length is appropriate for relationship
- [ ] Governing law jurisdiction is specified
- [ ] All checkboxes in template are completed
- [ ] HOOTNER-specific provisions (Section 10) are reviewed
- [ ] Both parties understand obligations
- [ ] Authorized signatories identified
- [ ] Disclosure log (Appendix B) is ready
- [ ] Internal tracking system updated
- [ ] Calendar reminders set for expiration
